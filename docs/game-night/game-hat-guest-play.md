# Game Hat Guest Play

## Overview

Game Hat should support participation by people who do not have their own Inner Circle account or device.

This allows flexible in-person social play.

---

## Core Principle

The game experience should not require every participant to install the app.

The system should support:
- shared devices
- guest participation
- flexible local play

---

## Shared Device Play

Multiple Players may participate using a single device.

Examples:
- family sharing one tablet
- party guests sharing a phone
- couch play scenarios

---

## Guest Participants

Future support may include temporary guest participants.

Guest participants may:
- join a session
- receive display names
- participate in gameplay

Guest participants may not require:
- permanent account creation
- Circle membership
- persistent profile storage

---

## Privacy Principles

Guest participation should:
- minimize unnecessary data collection
- avoid exposing unrelated Circle information
- remain session-scoped whenever possible

---

## Prompt Submission

Guest Players should still be able to:
- submit prompts
- participate in rounds
- receive scores

The system should still track:
- prompt ownership
- round participation
- score attribution

---

## Shared Device UI

Shared-device flows should prioritize:
- quick handoff between Players
- clear turn visibility
- privacy during prompt reveal
- minimal navigation complexity

---

## Active Player Privacy

When revealing prompts:
- non-active Players should not accidentally see prompts
- quick hide/reveal transitions may be useful
- device pass behavior should remain simple

---

## Future Possibilities

Possible future features:
- QR session joining
- temporary browser join
- cast-to-TV support
- companion display modes

---

## Design Principles

- low-friction participation
- socially comfortable device sharing
- lightweight onboarding
- preserve local multiplayer feel
