# Game Hat Audio and Feedback

## Overview

Game Hat should provide lightweight audio and visual feedback that improves clarity and social play without becoming distracting.

Feedback systems should support:
- gameplay readability
- timer awareness
- session pacing
- multiplayer synchronization

---

## Core Principle

Feedback should clarify gameplay state rather than overwhelm the experience.

Players should always understand:
- when rounds begin
- when timers are active
- when rounds end
- when important transitions occur

---

## Audio Categories

### Timer Audio

Possible sounds:
- countdown warning
- final seconds warning
- timer expiration

Purpose:
- maintain pacing
- reduce need to constantly watch screen

---

### State Transition Audio

Possible sounds:
- round start
- round completion
- player joined
- prompt submitted
- scoring confirmation

---

### Notification Audio

Possible future sounds:
- invite received
- reconnect success
- Host action alerts

---

## Visual Feedback

Visual feedback may include:
- countdown indicators
- active Player highlighting
- score updates
- phase transitions
- submission completion indicators

---

## Accessibility

Feedback systems should support:
- mute options
- reduced motion options
- visual alternatives to audio cues

Audio should never be the only gameplay indicator.

---

## Multiplayer Synchronization

Important audio cues should align closely with authoritative session timing.

Examples:
- timer expiration
- round start
- scoring transitions

---

## Design Philosophy

Audio and feedback should feel:
- lightweight
- friendly
- socially comfortable
- readable during live interaction

Avoid:
- loud arcade-style overload
- excessive sound spam
- distracting visual effects

---

## Future Possibilities

Possible future additions:
- customizable sound packs
- themed feedback styles
- accessibility presets
- haptic feedback support

---

## Design Principles

- clarity over spectacle
- synchronized feedback
- accessibility-aware design
- gameplay-first pacing
