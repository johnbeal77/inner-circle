# Game Hat History and Replay

## Overview

Game Hat may preserve lightweight historical information about completed sessions.

History systems should support:
- replayability
- social memory
- session summaries
- future statistics

without overcomplicating the MVP.

---

## Core Principle

History should enhance social experiences rather than create competitive pressure.

The MVP should prioritize:
- lightweight summaries
- replay convenience
- simple session continuity

---

## Session History

Completed sessions may preserve:
- session date
- participating Players
- final scores
- rounds played
- winning Players
- session settings

---

## Prompt History

Prompt tracking may preserve:
- used prompts
- skipped prompts
- prompt creators

Future rules may optionally:
- avoid recent prompt reuse
- track favorite prompts
- support prompt packs

---

## Replay Flow

After session completion:

Possible actions:
- replay same settings
- rematch with same Players
- return to lobby
- start new session

Replay should minimize setup friction.

---

## Statistics (Future)

Possible future statistics:
- rounds won
- participation frequency
- favorite modes
- average round time
- session history summaries

Statistics should remain lightweight and socially friendly.

---

## Privacy Principles

History should remain:
- Circle-scoped
- session-scoped when appropriate
- inaccessible to non-participants

Guest participation should avoid unnecessary long-term data retention.

---

## Competitive Philosophy

Game Hat is not intended to become a highly competitive ranked system.

History and statistics should:
- encourage fun
- preserve memories
- support replayability

Avoid:
- aggressive leaderboards
- toxic optimization pressure
- public ranking systems

---

## Future Possibilities

Possible future additions:
- favorite sessions
- exportable summaries
- highlight moments
- seasonal statistics
- custom prompt packs

---

## Design Principles

- lightweight persistence
- socially positive history
- replay convenience
- privacy-aware retention
- fun over competition
